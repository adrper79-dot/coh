# Cypher of Healing Doctrine vs Go-To-Market

## Core Doctrine

This is the permanent layer. It should remain stable even if channels, offers, or pricing change.

- The barber chair is a sanctuary of reflective care.
- Healing is a process of decoding inherited patterns and practicing restoration.
- The brand connects external care and internal dignity.
- Community matters; healing is not only solitary.
- The work is non-clinical unless and until delivered by properly licensed professionals.

## Go-To-Market Layer

This is the adaptable layer. It can change as the audience, business model, or maturity of the brand evolves.

- YouTube show and conversation format
- subscription model and pricing
- Patreon vs owned membership
- merch strategy
- Amazon vs direct web commerce mix
- launch sequencing
- funnel design
- email capture and nurture
- event packaging and upsells

## Rule for Decision-Making

If a decision changes the doctrine, it requires founder-level review.

If a decision only changes packaging, sequencing, pricing, or distribution, it belongs in go-to-market.

## Current Doctrine Statements to Preserve

- "Before the barber was a barber, he was a healer."
- "The outer is a reflection of the inner."
- The chair, course, products, and gatherings are one restoration system.
- The work is restorative and educational, not therapy.

## Current GTM Decisions Still Open

- where paid membership lives first
- how much content is free vs paid
- whether the show is positioned as interviews, barber-chair conversations, or guided reflections
- how books, bundles, and products ladder into higher-trust offers
- whether consultations are one-to-one guidance, wellness consulting, or a future post-licensure clinical offer
