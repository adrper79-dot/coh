# Cypher of Healing Experience Frameworks

## Purpose

This document records the frameworks used to translate the founder identity into the online experience so future changes stay coherent.

## 1. Brand Architecture

Use a branded house model.

- Master brand: Cypher of Healing
- Pathways: The Chair, The Vault, The Academy, The Show, The Stage, The Circle
- Rule: every pathway must clearly connect back to the same doctrine of reflective care and restoration

## 2. Progressive Disclosure

The experience should reveal information in this order:

1. What this is
2. Who it is for
3. What it is not
4. How to enter
5. Why to trust it
6. What to do next

Poetic language can support this structure, but it cannot replace it.

## 3. Trust Before Conversion

Use a trust ladder before asking for commitment.

- Orientation
- Doctrine
- Boundaries and disclaimer
- Proof and examples
- Offer selection
- Conversion

## 4. Jobs To Be Done

Map each pathway to a primary visitor job.

- The Chair: I need a trusted, embodied place to begin.
- The Academy: I need a structured way to understand my patterns.
- The Vault: I need tools that keep the work present in daily life.
- The Show: I need a low-friction way to understand the worldview and voice.
- The Stage: I want communal learning and live reflection.

## 5. Compliance as UX

Compliance language is not only legal hygiene. It is part of user orientation.

- clarify non-therapy boundaries early
- avoid clinical implication in CTAs and offer naming
- distinguish education, reflective care, and community from treatment

## 6. Offer-Ladder Discipline

Organize CTAs from lower-friction to higher-commitment.

1. Read or watch
2. Attend or explore
3. Buy or enroll
4. Book or apply

## 7. Copy Standard

Every page should answer these questions within the first screen or two:

- What is this pathway?
- Who is it for?
- What outcome should I expect?
- What boundary should I understand?

## 8. Engineering Standard

- keep routes explicit and discoverable
- preserve one shared visual language across new surfaces
- prefer minimal, additive changes over broad redesign churn
- validate each frontend slice with a production build after edits
